What Growing Tomatoes Taught Me About Trading

By Brian Eboso | October 10, 2025 | Mindset, Trading Psychology

I never thought growing tomatoes would fix my trading. But it did.

Four weeks ago, I planted four MoneyMaker tomato seedlings in a sack. No fancy greenhouse. No irrigation system. Just soil, sun, water, and time.

As I watched them grow slowly - painfully slowly - something clicked.

The same discipline I needed for those plants was the exact discipline I was missing in my trades.

Same principle. Different soil.

Let me show you what I mean.

The First Week: When Nothing Happens (And That's Okay)

In farming: I planted those seedlings and stared at them every day. Nothing. No visible growth. Just... waiting.

My impatient brain said: "Maybe I should dig them up and check if they're rooting properly? Maybe add more fertilizer? Maybe replant them somewhere else?"

But I knew better. Growth happens underground before you see it above ground. Rushing the process would kill them.

In trading: I mark my three weekly levels every Sunday (previous week's close, high, low). Then Monday comes. No setup. Tuesday comes. Still nothing.

My impatient brain used to say: "Maybe I should trade a different pair? Maybe switch to day trading? Maybe the system isn't working this week?"

But I know better now. Structure forms when it's ready, not when I'm bored. Forcing a trade outside my system is like digging up seeds to "help them grow faster."

üå± Lesson #1: You Can't Force Growth

Whether it's tomatoes or trades, the best thing you can do is wait. Create the right conditions (mark your levels, follow your system), then let nature - or the market - do its work.

Week Two: Resist the Urge to "Do Something"

By week two, my tomato plants had tiny leaves starting to unfold. Progress, but slow.

Every farmer knows the temptation: overwatering.

"If a little water is good, more must be better, right?"

Wrong. Overwatering drowns roots. It rots the plant from the bottom up. You're trying to help, but you're actually killing it.

Trading has the same trap: overtrading.

I've been there. System says "wait for weekly close rejection." But the chart is moving. FOMO kicks in. I enter a trade that doesn't meet my criteria because "it MIGHT work."

That's overwatering. That's drowning your account with impulsive decisions.

In farming, you water when needed - not every hour because you're anxious.

In trading, you trade when your setup appears - not every day because you're bored.

Week Three: The Invisible Progress Phase

This was the hardest week. The plants looked... the same. Maybe slightly bigger? Hard to tell.

I started second-guessing everything:

  • "Is the soil wrong?"
  • "Should I have used a different variety?"
  • "Am I wasting my time?"

But here's what I couldn't see: the root system was exploding underground.

Roots were spreading, strengthening, preparing the plant for rapid growth. None of that was visible from the surface.

Trading has these invisible progress phases too.

There were weeks (many weeks) where I followed my system perfectly:

  • Marked my levels ‚úÖ
  • Waited for setups ‚úÖ
  • Followed entry criteria ‚úÖ
  • Managed risk properly ‚úÖ

But... no good setups appeared. Market was choppy. I didn't trade.

Zero profit that week. But massive growth happened.

Why? Because I was building discipline. Strengthening my "trading roots" - the habits that would support me when big opportunities came.

üí° The Non-Trade is a Trade

Every time you DON'T take a bad trade, you're making progress. You're strengthening your system. You're building roots.

The market will reward you for this... eventually. Just like those tomato roots rewarded me in week four.

Week Four: When Patience Pays Off

This is where the magic happened.

Between week three and week four, my tomato plants exploded. Suddenly they were twice the height. Leaves everywhere. Strong stems. The transformation was dramatic.

All that invisible underground work (the roots) finally showed up above ground.

Watch the progress here:

Look at those plants. Four weeks ago, they were tiny seedlings I wasn't even sure would survive.

Now? They're thriving.

What changed? Nothing.

I didn't switch strategies. I didn't panic. I didn't overwater or dig them up to "check progress."

I trusted the process. Watered consistently. Protected them from harsh conditions. And waited.

My trading had the same breakthrough.

After weeks of following my Weekly Close Reaction System with discipline (even when it felt like "nothing was happening"), the big setups finally came.

October 13th:

  • Weekly close rejection formed perfectly
  • All criteria met
  • I entered with confidence because I'd been patient
  • Result: +507 pips, $5.07 profit ‚úÖ

That one trade rewarded all the weeks I spent NOT trading. All the times I closed my chart because no setup appeared. All the discipline I built when it felt like "nothing was working."

üåæ Lesson #3: Breakthroughs Come After Consistency

Whether it's farming or trading, explosive growth happens AFTER boring consistency.

You don't see the roots forming.
You don't see the discipline building.
You don't see the progress...

...until suddenly, you do.

The Parallel That Changed Everything

Here's what growing tomatoes taught me about trading:

üå± In Farming

  • Plant seeds at the right time
  • Water consistently, not constantly
  • Protect from harsh conditions
  • Trust the growth process
  • Don't dig up to "check progress"
  • Harvest when ready, not before
  • Some plants fail - that's nature
  • Patience beats panic every time

üìä In Trading

  • Mark levels at the right time (Sunday)
  • Trade consistently, not constantly
  • Use stop losses to protect capital
  • Trust your system's edge
  • Don't check charts obsessively
  • Exit when targets hit, not early
  • Some trades lose - that's markets
  • Patience beats panic every time

Same principle. Different soil.

What About You?

If you're reading this, maybe you're struggling with the same things I struggled with:

  • Impatience when the market is quiet
  • Overtrading because you feel like you "should be doing something"
  • Doubting your system during invisible progress phases
  • Wanting results NOW instead of trusting the process

I get it. I've been there. Hell, I'm STILL there some weeks.

But here's what helps me: I look at my tomato plants.

They remind me that growth takes time. That consistency beats intensity. That you can't force the market any more than you can force a seed to sprout.

You can only create the right conditions and wait.

How to Apply the Farming Mindset to Your Trading

Here are the specific actions that work for me:

üåæ Your Trading Garden Checklist:

1. Plant Your Seeds (Sunday Prep)

Mark your three weekly levels. This is planting. Everything else grows from here.

2. Water Consistently (Check for Setups Mon-Wed)

Check if setups are forming. But don't obsess. Once in morning, once in evening. That's enough.

3. Protect from Storms (Use Stop Losses Always)

Stop losses are your fence. They protect your capital from unexpected market moves.

4. Don't Dig Up Your Seeds (Trust the Process)

If no setup appears by Wednesday, close your chart. Don't force a trade. Growth is happening even when you can't see it.

5. Harvest When Ready (Exit at Targets)

When price hits your target (previous week's high/low), take profit. Don't get greedy. You'll plant again next week.
