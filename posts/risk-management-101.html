Protect Your Capital: Risk Management 101

By Brian Eboso | October 15, 2025 | Risk Management, Trading Psychology

You can have the best trading strategy in the world, but without proper risk management, you'll still blow your account.

I learned this the hard way. Not once, but multiple times over 4+ years of losses.

The problem wasn't my strategy. It was how I managed (or failed to manage) risk. I'd risk 10% on a "sure thing" trade, watch it go against me, panic, and either hold hoping it would reverse or exit too early on winners.

Sound familiar?

Trading is like farming - you don't plant all your seeds in one patch and hope for rain. You spread them out, protect against storms, and harvest only when ready. Risk management is the fence around your capital, ensuring you survive market volatility to trade another day.

Let me share the exact risk management principles that transformed my trading from reckless gambling to disciplined execution.

1. Why Most Traders Fail (It's Not What You Think)

Here's a brutal truth: You don't need to be right more than 50% of the time to be profitable.

Let that sink in.

You can lose on 6 out of 10 trades and still make money - IF you manage risk properly.

Example:

  • 10 trades with 40% win rate (4 wins, 6 losses)
  • Risk: $10 per trade
  • Reward: $30 per winning trade (3:1 risk/reward)
  • Result: 4 wins √ó $30 = $120 profit | 6 losses √ó $10 = $60 loss | Net: +$60 (60% return)

‚ö†Ô∏è The Reality Check:

Most traders do the opposite - they risk $30 to make $10, and even with a 60% win rate, they're losing money. Small wins, big losses. That's the path to a blown account.

Risk management isn't about avoiding losses. It's about controlling losses so your winners can shine.

2. The Golden Rules I Wish Someone Told Me Day One

After years of trial and error (mostly error), here are the non-negotiable rules that saved my trading career:

Rule #1: Never Risk More Than 1-2% Per Trade

This is THE foundational rule. Period.

Why? Because losing streaks happen. Even with a great system, you might lose 5-7 trades in a row. That's normal variance.

What happens at different risk levels:

  • 1% risk: 10 losses in a row = -10% drawdown (recoverable)
  • 5% risk: 10 losses in a row = -40% drawdown (devastating)
  • 10% risk: 10 losses in a row = -65% drawdown (account crippled)

üí° My Personal Rule:

I risk exactly 1.5% per trade on my Weekly Close Reaction System. On a $1,000 account, that's $15 per trade. On a $10,000 account, it's $150. Simple. Scalable. Sustainable.

Rule #2: Always Use Stop Losses (No Exceptions)

A stop loss is not optional. It's not a suggestion. It's survival.

Where to place stop losses on weekly structure trades:

  • For rejection setups: Place stop loss 10-20 pips beyond the rejection wick (depending on volatility)
  • For breakout setups: Place stop loss 20-30 pips below the broken weekly close
  • Never move stop loss FURTHER away after entry (that's hoping, not trading)
  • You CAN move stop loss to breakeven once trade is in profit

üö® The Stop Loss Trap:

"But Brian, the market always hits my stop loss then goes my way!"

This is confirmation bias. You remember those times vividly, but forget the dozens of times your stop loss saved you from -50% losses. Stop losses are insurance - you pay a small premium (the loss) to protect against catastrophic damage.

Rule #3: Position Sizing is Everything

This is where most traders mess up, even when they know about the 1-2% rule.

Here's the formula (it's simpler than you think):

Position Size Formula:

Position Size (lots) = (Account Risk in $) √∑ (Stop Loss in pips √ó Pip Value)

Real Example from My October 13 Trade:

  • Account size: $1,000
  • Risk per trade: 1.5% = $15
  • Entry: 1.16183 (SELL EUR/USD)
  • Stop loss: 1.16014 (17 pips away)
  • Pip value: $0.10 per pip (for 0.01 lot on EUR/USD)
  • Calculation: $15 √∑ (17 pips √ó $0.10) = $15 √∑ $1.70 = 8.8 ‚Üí Round down to 0.08 lots

But I trade with 0.01 lot (smaller position) because I'm conservative. The point is: your position size should match your stop loss distance.

Every trade should target AT LEAST 2√ó what you're risking. Ideally 3:1 or better.

Why? Because you need your winners to cover your losers with room to spare.

3. My 5-Step Risk Management Checklist (Before Every Trade)

Here's exactly what I do before clicking "Buy" or "Sell":

Pre-Trade Checklist:

Step 1: Confirm Setup Criteria Met

Is this a valid weekly close rejection on 2H chart? Did it meet all system criteria?

Step 2: Mark Entry & Stop Loss

Entry = rejection close. Stop = 10-20 pips beyond wick. Write it down.

Step 3: Calculate Position Size

Use calculator. Match position to 1.5% risk. Confirm before entry.

Step 4: Identify Profit Targets

Target = Previous week's high/low. Confirm R:R is at least 2:1. If not, skip.

Step 5: Place Stop Loss IMMEDIATELY

The moment you enter, set your stop. Not "in a minute." Right. Now.

If any step fails, I don't trade. No exceptions. No "this time is different."

4. The Biggest Risk Management Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

These are the mistakes I made repeatedly. Don't be me:

Mistake #1: Moving Stop Loss Further Away

The situation: Trade goes against you. You think "just give it more room." You move stop loss 50 pips further.

The result: Bigger loss. More pain. Regret.

The fix: If your original stop was hit, the setup was invalid. Take the small loss and move on. You can ONLY move stop loss CLOSER (to breakeven or trailing), never further.

Mistake #2: Revenge Trading After a Loss

The situation: You take a loss. Immediately look for another trade to "make it back."

The result: Impulsive entry. Poor setup. Another loss. Spiral continues.

The fix: After a loss, close MT5 for at least 2 hours. Go for a walk. Journal the trade. Process the emotion. Then reassess.

Mistake #3: Risking More on "High Confidence" Trades

The situation: Setup looks perfect. You think "I'll risk 5% this time - it's a sure thing!"

The result: Market doesn't care about your confidence. Trade fails. 5% gone.

The fix: Every trade gets the same risk: 1-2%. No exceptions. Confidence is not a risk management strategy.

5. The Farming Mindset: Patience Protects Profit

Growing tomatoes taught me something crucial: you can't force growth, but you can protect what you plant.

Watch my farming journey here: Patience in Action - My Tomato Farm

In farming:

  • You plant seeds (capital allocation)
  • You water consistently (disciplined execution)
  • You protect from pests (stop losses)
  • You wait for harvest (patience)
  • Some crops fail - that's expected (losses happen)

In trading:

  • You allocate 1-2% per trade (seeds)
  • You follow your system every week (watering)
  • You use stop losses to protect capital (pest control)
  • You let structure play out (waiting for harvest)
  • Some trades lose - that's part of the process

Same principle. Different soil.
